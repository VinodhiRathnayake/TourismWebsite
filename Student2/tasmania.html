Bridestowe Lavender Estate


Nestled in Tasmania, Australia, Bridestowe Lavender Estate captivates visitors with its breathtaking Lavandula angustifolia fields. During peak blooming season (December to January), it transforms into a fragrant purple paradise. The air is filled with the sweet scent of lavender, creating an enchanting ambiance.

Visitors stroll through the vast lavender fields, savoring the soothing crunch underfoot and gentle rustle of blooms in the breeze. The estate offers engaging activities, including witnessing the distillation process in the on-site distillery. Bridestowe Lavender Estate is a testament to the harmonious blend of human and natural beauty, providing an unforgettable experience in nature's embrace.

Russell Falls


The cascading waterfalls, divided into two tiers, create a mesmerizing display amid the ancient rainforest. The park's well-maintained trails lead visitors through lush temperate rainforests, adorned with ferns and vibrant flora. Established in 1916, Russell Falls holds historical and ecological significance, symbolizing Tasmania's commitment to preservation.

As one of the state's oldest national parks, it provides a sanctuary for diverse wildlife, including the elusive platypus and Tasmanian pademelon. A true gem of Tasmania, Russell Falls enchants all who seek solace and wonder amidst nature's timeless beauty.

Port Arthur Historic Site


Port Arthur Historic Site, a remote Tasmanian landmark founded in 1830 by the British Empire, holds significance as a penal colony. Today, it's a popular tourist destination, offering insights into prisoner and guard lives through well-preserved structures and guided tours. Tragedy struck in 1996, claiming 35 lives, commemorated by on-site memorials.

Despite its dark past, Port Arthur endures as a compelling testament to Australia's history, inviting visitors to reflect on its heritage and cherish its remarkable legacy.

Cradle Mountain-Lake


Cradle Mountain-Lake St. Clair National Park, located in the Central Highlands, showcases Tasmania's stunning landscapes, featuring serene lakes nestled amid majestic mountains.

The park's northern part boasts renowned peaks like Cradle Mountain, while the southern area around Lake St. Clair is adorned with reflective lakes. Deep river gorges meander through the alpine scenery, accompanied by lush rainforests and blooming meadows. Abundant wildlife, including wombats, echidnas, and Tasmanian devils, enhances the park's allure. Hikers are drawn to the park's diverse natural wonders, exploring a well-maintained network of paths and trails that offer an unforgettable experience in this pristine wilderness.
