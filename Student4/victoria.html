Phillip Island


Phillip Island is located just off Australia’s southern coast, which is about two hours' drive from Melbourne. There's so much to see and do on Phillip Island, no matter what time of year you visit! Discover adventure attractions, family fun activities, tours, scenic flights, and world-class motorsports. Its most popular tourist attraction is Penguin Parade, which is home to the world's tiniest penguins, the Little Penguins. Visitors can sit on the normal viewing platform, the improved penguin plus platform, or even the underground viewing platform to watch these penguins. Visitors can hang out near the rocks from the overlook and watch seals sunbake away the day. Visitors can enjoy a day on the water by going on a boat trip or a fishing charter! The Chocolate Factory on Phillip Island, which honors all that chocolate has to offer, is without a doubt the island's tastiest attraction.Chocolate lovers shouldn't skip this tasty stop!

Melbourne zoo


Melbourne Zoo is well-known for being one of the best parks in Melbourne, providing lovely attractions and activities to its visitors, particularly families. When you venture deeper into Melbourne Zoo, you'll discover a special area set aside for wild animals, which is home to several spectacular creatures. Continue with your trip to the predators zone where you can have fun observing a variety of hazardous animals like wolves and lions. You could stop by the area that features a range of attractive bird species such as Jabiru, Cattle Egret, and Blue Winged Kookaburra. You could see a variety of amazing raptors including Black Eagles and Masked Owls. Explore an aquatic world showcasing Victorian tiny penguins, koalas and kangaroos. Visitors can view historical cages, such as the heritage-listed Elephant House, which has been made for use by customers who pay to sleep overnight in tents at the zoo during popular Roar and Snore evenings. These evenings provide the public with the opportunity to see some of the zoo's nocturnal creatures through evening guided tours led by professional camp hosts.

National Gallery of Victoria


The National Gallery of Victoria (NGV) is the oldest and most visited gallery in Australia which is situated over two magnificent buildings – NGV International and NGV Australia. The Gallery presents over 40 exhibitions a year including major international blockbusters and a wide range of exhibitions, programs, and events. The NGV is home to the most important art collection in the area, a massive collection of more than 70,000 pieces spanning thousands of years and representing a wide range of ideas, genres, and styles. The collection includes treasures from ancient Egypt, Greece, Rome, and the pre-Columbian period, as well as an Oceanic gallery dedicated to Pacific indigenous cultures. You will also find an outstanding collection of European and British paintings dating from 1200 to today, fashion and textile exhibits, photography, furniture, and sculpture. The NGV provides a remarkable visual arts experience through a variety of temporary exhibitions, collection displays, talks, tours, children's programs, films, late-night events, and performances.

Puffing Billy Train


Step back in time at Australia’s oldest and best-preserved heritage steam railway, Puffing Billy, situated in the picturesque Dandenong Ranges. The main starting point is Belgrave Station, which serves as the administrative and operational hub for the railway. The Puffing Billy Railway is a great place to take in the fresh air as the train travels through the temperate rainforest, with lush fern gullies brushing past the open side carriages and Mountain Ash trees towering overhead. Discover fascinating fauna and stunning landscapes ripe for exploration while sitting on the carriage sill of one of Puffing Billy's open-side carriages. Passengers can also explore the Menzies Creek Museum. The Dandenong Ranges, are made up of several flowing waterfalls, undulating hills, and steep valleys. Once the Train has arrived at Menzies Creek, the tour continues in Sassafras. You will be able to go through stunning thick rainforests in Sassafras, which are home to a variety of species, including Wallabies and Lyre Birds. There are a few antique and craft shops in town where you can pick up a treasured souvenir! After having enjoyed the Puffing Billy Day Tour's journey, we then go back to Melbourne.
